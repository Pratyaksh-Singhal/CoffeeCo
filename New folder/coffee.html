Types of Coffee

Espresso

Espresso is a coffee-brewing method of Italian origin,in which a small amount of nearly boiling water is forced under 9–10 bars of pressure through finely-ground coffee beans.

Caffè Americano

Caffè Americano is a type of coffee drink prepared by diluting an espresso with hot water, giving it a similar strength to, but different flavor from, traditionally brewed coffee.

Frappuccino

Frappuccino is a line of blended iced coffee drinks sold by Starbucks. It consists of coffee or crème base, blended with ice and ingredients such as flavored syrups and usually topped with whipped cream and or spices.

Cappuccino

A cappuccino is an espresso-based coffee drink and is traditionally prepared with steamed milk foam. Variations of the drink involve the use of cream instead of milk and flavoring with cinnamon.

Mocha

A caffè mocha is a chocolate-flavoured warm beverage that is a variant of a caffè latte commonly served in a glass rather than a mug

Ristretto

Ristretto is made of a more highly concentrated espresso coffee. It is made with the same amount of ground coffee, but extracted with a finer grind using half as much water.

Irish

Irish coffee is a caffeinated alcoholic drink consisting of Irish whiskey, hot coffee and sugar, which has been stirred and topped with cream. The coffee is drunk through the cream.

Affogato

An affogato is an Italian coffee-based dessert. It usually takes the form of a scoop of plain milk-flavored or vanilla gelato or ice cream topped or "drowned" with a shot of hot espresso.
